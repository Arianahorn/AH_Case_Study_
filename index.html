User Research and Stakeholder Interview

Starting an RWD for an excisting Website starts with the right approach. Every step of USER RESEARCH has to be covered. When we started to redesign "Teach With Africa's Website" we informed us about competitors and started to contect clients and stakeholder. We figured out that this Non-Profit had 2 direct and a couple of indirect competiots, we decided to show the Client/Stakeholder the websites from the direct competetors after we got in touch with them. We also wrote down an interview plan to get informaton about "Teach With Africa".
After the interview we were able to start over to next steps.

Heuristic Evaluation and Usability Tests

Following we decided to rate the excisting website with a heurisitc evaluation template. First, it was important for us to understand the differences to competitors and on the other hand to filter out the pain-points of style, navigation and understanding for customers. What do we need to focuse on when we start with the new RWD and what do we need to understand from a customers view. Out of the usability inspection we created 3 usability tasks and an user interview plan.

3 TASKS:

  • Is the user able to understand the purpose of teachwithafrica.org
  • Is the userl able to apply to one of the main programs
  • Is the user able to Donate to TWA or volunteer

When it comes ot a website, which is completely confusing in it's interface design, it is important to use a heuristic evaluation template to identify problems.

User Persona and Problem Statement

Elena, a recent graduate, needs an intuitive and convincing website to feel safe and informed to apply for a teacher program with Teach with Africa.

Problem Statement
Education shouldn't be a privilege, it should be a right. Many countries suffer from a lack of resources and aren't able to offer a proper education. Teach with Africa is a non-profit organization that provides a path for educators to engage with and contribute to communities in South Africa. After our research, we discovered that rather than encouraging educators to pursue their career goals, the TWA website discourages educators from enrolling in the program.

User Insight: How might we help encourage Elena and other educators to participate in this progam and provide a seamless website user experience as a goal.

Lofi Prototype

As a team but with an individual content of each work member, we created a taskflow and out of this the lofi wireframes web and mobile. Together we decided what design elements we figured out as the best working ones. After that we created our figma prototype, we started with some onboarding usability tests, because of the tests we were able to fix unclear navigation elements, or missing buttons etc.
Our main objectives were to see if users could easily navigate through the flow of a first time user Applying for a program and donate for the couse.

User Feedback: Hamburger menu wasn't catching their eye without giving hints and they missed parts like a confirmation screen

Style Guide

We wanted to keep our font choices & button designs easy to read, but still visually engaging for our users.
Our color palette was inspired by the logo of Teach With Africa, which contains the colors of the South African flag.

Final steps:
In our redesign, the goal was to make the website navigation intuitive and simple, while presenting content using a sleek, clean design. When we started this project we realized how hard it was to understand what the actual mission of this non-profit organization is. So many questions and it was by far a challenging and broad content to narrow down to an user fully selfunderstanding onboarding.
Stakeholder and Clients were the entire time included in our progress of the redesign. They recived a fully based on their wishes and interests customized template. Even though they weren't able to take the design over now, they still consider to get a webdeveloper team to code it and publish the new design.
